10-Day Grand Tour · Aug 27 – Sep 6, 2025

Five Cities,
One Unforgettable Journey

Amsterdam · Cologne · Swiss Alps · Paris · Brussels · Amsterdam

Amsterdam → Cologne → Swiss Alps → Paris → Brussels → Amsterdam Arrive Aug 27 · Depart Sep 6 at 11:00
Day 01

Amsterdam — The Essential Day

Wed, Aug 27 Amsterdam

Schedule

Morning
10:20

✈ Arrive at Schiphol (AMS)

Clear customs, collect bags. Sprinter train to Amsterdam Centraal (~17 min, €5.50). Check in or drop bags at hotel — most allow early luggage drop. Grab a coffee and get moving.

☕Breakfast: Scandinavian Embassy — the city's best specialty coffee
Afternoon
12:30

Jordaan, Canals & the Big Highlights

  • Anne Frank House — book timed entry in advance (annefrank.org), absolutely non-negotiable
  • Prinsengracht & Keizersgracht canal walk — the classic Amsterdam scene
  • Rijksmuseum — Rembrandt, Vermeer, Dutch Golden Age; allow 90 min (pre-book)
  • Albert Cuyp Market in De Pijp — stroopwafels, street food, atmosphere
🥩Lunch: Moeders — hearty Dutch comfort food, Jordaan 🐟Snack: raw herring at a Haring cart
Evening
19:00

Canal Cruise & Leidseplein

  • Evening canal cruise — golden-hour light on the grachten is unmissable
  • Leidseplein square — café terraces, street performers, great energy
  • Brouwerij 't IJ — windmill craft brewery, a perfect nightcap
🍽Dinner: De Silveren Spiegel — refined Dutch cuisine in a 1614 canal house

Tip: One focused day is exactly enough. Prioritise Anne Frank House + Rijksmuseum + a canal cruise and you'll leave satisfied. Buy an OV-chipkaart for trams and buses.

Day 02

Amsterdam → Cologne

Thu, Aug 28 Cologne
🚄

Amsterdam Centraal → Köln Hauptbahnhof

ICE high-speed train · Book via NS International or DB · depart ~08:30

~2 hr 40 min

Schedule

Morning
08:30

Depart Amsterdam, Arrive Cologne ~11:10

The Cathedral is literally outside the station exit — you'll see it before you're even through the doors. Drop bags at hotel and walk straight there.

Afternoon
11:30

Kölner Dom & Altstadt

  • Kölner Dom — climb the South Tower (533 steps) for Rhine panoramas
  • Roman-Germanic Museum — ancient mosaics and Roman Cologne
  • Hohenzollern Bridge — love locks and great river views
  • Rhine promenade walk toward the Old Town
🍺Lunch: Früh am Dom — order Kölsch in traditional 0.2L Stange glasses
Afternoon
15:30

Museum Ludwig & Belgian Quarter

  • Museum Ludwig — excellent modern art: Picasso, Warhol, German Expressionism
  • Belgisches Viertel — indie boutiques, street art, lively café culture
Evening
19:00

Rhine Sunset & Dinner

  • Hohenzollern Bridge at dusk — beautiful light on the water
🥩Dinner: Em Krützche — Rhine-view restaurant, try Himmel un Ääd (potato & blood sausage)

Tip: The Cologne Card gives unlimited transit + museum discounts. Grab one at the station tourist office on arrival.

Day 03

Cologne → Interlaken Alps Base

Fri, Aug 29 Swiss Alps
🚄

Köln Hbf → Basel → Interlaken Ost

ICE to Basel, then SBB InterCity through the Bernese Oberland · Book via DB + sbb.ch · depart ~08:00

~5 hr 30 min

Schedule

Morning
08:00

Depart Cologne

The leg from Basel onward is one of Europe's most scenic train rides — you enter the Bernese Oberland and the Alps unfold around you. Arrive Interlaken Ost ~13:30. Interlaken is your mountain base for three nights.

Afternoon
14:00

Interlaken & Lake Thun

  • Höheweg promenade — the famous strip between Lakes Thun and Brienz with the Eiger, Mönch and Jungfrau directly ahead
  • Boat cruise on Lake Thun — glassy water, Alpine reflections, castle silhouettes
  • Spiez village (optional short stop) — beautiful lakeside castle
🧀Lunch: Käseschnitte (Swiss cheese toast) at a lakeside café
Evening
19:00

Settle In — Plan the Alps Days

Easy first evening. Check the Jungfraujoch webcam (jungfrau.ch), review tomorrow's route, set an early alarm, and rest. The mountains demand early starts.

🍷Dinner: Restaurant Schuh — elegant terrace overlooking the Höheweg

Tip: The Jungfrau Travel Pass covers regional trains, boats and cable cars across the region — compare it against point-to-point tickets for your specific days, as it can save a substantial amount.

Day 04

Jungfraujoch — Top of Europe 3,454 m

Sat, Aug 30 Swiss Alps

Schedule

Morning
07:00

Early Departure — First Trains Are Best

Train from Interlaken Ost → Grindelwald or Lauterbrunnen → Kleine Scheidegg → Jungfraujoch. The rack railway through the Eiger tunnel is an experience on its own. Allow 2.5 hours each way.

  • Target the ~07:35 departure — best visibility, fewest crowds, best light
  • Book at jungfrau.ch in advance — CHF 145–215 depending on season and pass
☕Breakfast on the train or at Kleine Scheidegg station
Late Morn
10:00

At the Summit (3,454 m)

  • Sphinx Observatory — 360° panorama over the Aletsch Glacier, the longest glacier in the Alps
  • Snow Fun Park — sledging and snowball fights in August, extraordinary novelty
  • Ice Palace — carved glacier tunnels with ice sculptures
  • Plateau Trail — walking path along the snow with views of peaks and ice in all directions
🍜Lunch: Crystal Restaurant at the top — pricey but the setting is extraordinary
Afternoon
14:00

Descent via Grindelwald

  • Return via Grindelwald — gorgeous village directly under the Eiger north face
  • Stroll the village, pick up local cheese, browse the shops
  • Grindelwald First cable car (optional, extra fee) — clifftop walks and mountain cart rides
Evening
19:30

Return to Interlaken

Back in town by evening. Rest well — Schilthorn tomorrow is another big day.

🥩Dinner: Laterne Restaurant — warm, unpretentious Swiss mountain food

Tip: Check the Jungfraujoch live webcam the morning you go. If the summit is socked in with cloud, swap Day 4 and Day 5 — Schilthorn at a lower elevation often has better visibility on overcast days.

Day 05

Schilthorn & Lauterbrunnen Valley 2,970 m

Sun, Aug 31 Swiss Alps

Schedule

Morning
08:00

Lauterbrunnen Valley

  • Train from Interlaken Ost to Lauterbrunnen (~20 min)
  • Staubbach Falls — 300 m waterfall plunging straight off the cliff into the valley, free and breathtaking
  • Trümmelbach Falls — glacial meltwater carving through the mountain interior (CHF ~14 at the door)
  • Walk the valley floor trail between waterfalls — the valley has 72 of them
🥐Breakfast: Airtime Café, Lauterbrunnen — great views, good coffee
Midday
11:30

Schilthorn — Piz Gloria (2,970 m)

  • Cable car from Stechelberg via Gimmelwald and Mürren to the summit
  • Piz Gloria revolving restaurant — filming location for the 1969 James Bond film On Her Majesty's Secret Service
  • Panorama: Eiger, Mönch, Jungfrau, and on clear days all the way to Mont Blanc and the Black Forest
  • James Bond World exhibition — fun interactive displays about the filming
🍽Lunch: Piz Gloria rotating restaurant — one slow revolution while you eat
Afternoon
15:00

Mürren — Car-Free Clifftop Village

  • Stop at Mürren on the descent — one of Switzerland's most beautiful villages, clinging to a cliff at 1,650 m, no cars ever
  • Northface Trail — a 4 km walk with the Eiger's legendary north face filling the entire skyline
  • Sit on a bench, breathe the mountain air — this is the Switzerland you came for
  • Optional: walk from Mürren down to Gimmelwald (30 min, one of Europe's great short hikes)
Evening
19:00

Final Alps Evening in Interlaken

Return to Interlaken. Pack for Paris tomorrow. If skies are clear, sit outside — watch the Eiger and Jungfrau turn pink at alpenglow. It's a moment you'll remember.

🫕Farewell fondue: Restaurant Tälli, Interlaken — end on a classic

Tip: August is peak paragliding season in Interlaken — if that's on your bucket list, operators in Lauterbrunnen can arrange tandem jumps with landing in the Interlaken valley. Unforgettable views on the way down.

Day 06

Interlaken → Paris

Mon, Sep 1 Paris
🚄

Interlaken Ost → Bern → Paris Gare de Lyon

SBB IC to Bern, then TGV Lyria · Book via sbb.ch + SNCF Connect · depart ~08:00

~5 hr 30 min

Schedule

Morning
08:00

Depart Interlaken

Train to Bern (~50 min), connect to TGV Lyria through the Jura to Paris Gare de Lyon. Arrive ~13:30. Check in, freshen up.

Afternoon
14:30

Left Bank First Impressions

  • Notre-Dame de Paris — reconstruction ongoing, still striking to see in person
  • Île Saint-Louis — charming island, Berthillon ice cream (legendary)
  • Luxembourg Gardens — peaceful stroll, pull up a metal chair by the fountain
🥐Café crème and a croissant at a Left Bank terrace — your Paris welcome
Evening
19:30

Eiffel Tower at Dusk

  • Trocadéro terrace — the classic Eiffel Tower view at golden hour
  • Champ de Mars for the 22:00 light show (sparkles on the hour after dark)
🍷Dinner: Au Pied de Fouet — tiny classic Parisian bistro, honest prices 🦞Splurge: Le Jules Verne inside the Tower — book far ahead
Day 07

Paris — Grands Musées & Montmartre

Tue, Sep 2 Paris

Schedule

Morning
09:00

The Louvre

  • Mona Lisa, Venus de Milo, Winged Victory — allow at least 2–3 hours
  • Book at ticketlouvre.fr — mandatory, no walk-up tickets in peak season
  • Cour Napoléon glass pyramid for photos before going in
☕Breakfast: Café Marly — terrace looking directly at the pyramid
Afternoon
13:30

Musée d'Orsay & Saint-Germain

  • Musée d'Orsay — Monet, Renoir, Degas in a spectacular converted railway station
  • Saint-Germain-des-Prés stroll — Sartre and de Beauvoir's old neighbourhood
  • Rue de Buci market — cheese, charcuterie, wine for a spontaneous picnic
🥗Lunch: Café de Flore or Les Deux Magots — order slowly, watch the world go by
Evening
18:30

Montmartre by Night

  • Sacré-Cœur Basilica — panoramic Paris at dusk from the steps
  • Place du Tertre — artists painting under warm lamplight
  • Le Moulin Rouge exterior — quintessentially Parisian street scene
🧅Dinner: La Bonne Franquette — French onion soup in a Montmartre institution

Tip: The Paris Museum Pass (2 or 4 days) covers the Louvre, Orsay, Versailles, and 50+ sites — great value if you're doing multiple museum days.

Day 08

Versailles & Le Marais

Wed, Sep 3 Paris

Schedule

Morning
08:30

Palace of Versailles Day Trip

  • RER C from Pont de l'Alma → Versailles Rive Gauche (~40 min, ~€7 return)
  • Hall of Mirrors, Grand Apartments, King's Private Apartments
  • Gardens — Le Nôtre's geometric masterwork; Grand Canal boat ride (optional)
  • Arrive at 09:00 opening to beat the tour groups
🥐Pack pastries from your hotel — eat in the gardens, far cheaper and more enjoyable
Afternoon
15:00

Le Marais

  • Place des Vosges — Paris's oldest royal square with beautiful arcades
  • Musée Picasso (optional — excellent collection if you love his work)
  • Rue des Rosiers — Jewish Quarter, legendary falafel
  • Marché des Enfants Rouges — Paris's oldest covered market (1615)
🥙Falafel: L'As du Fallafel on Rue des Rosiers — always queued, always worth it
Evening
19:30

Seine Cruise & Last Paris Night

  • Vedettes du Pont-Neuf evening cruise — city lit up at night is magical
  • Final Paris dinner
🥂Bouillon Chartier — Art Nouveau brasserie, wonderful food at bistro prices 🍾Nightcap: Bar Hemingway at the Ritz — a splurge absolutely worth making once
Day 09

Paris → Brussels

Thu, Sep 4 Brussels
🚄

Paris Gare du Nord → Bruxelles-Midi

Thalys high-speed · Book via thalys.com or Rail Europe · depart ~10:00

~1 hr 25 min

Schedule

Morning
10:00

Relaxed Paris Departure

Final croissant, board the Thalys. Arrive Brussels Midi by ~12:00. Drop bags and walk 10 minutes to Grand-Place — start immediately.

Afternoon
12:30

Grand-Place & Historic Centre

  • Grand-Place — one of Europe's truly great public squares, UNESCO listed; stop and stare
  • Manneken Pis — tiny, beloved, always in a themed costume
  • Galeries Royales Saint-Hubert — ornate 19th-century covered arcade
  • Magritte Museum — dedicated to Belgium's great surrealist painter
🍟Lunch: Moules-frites at Aux Armes de Bruxelles — the definitive Belgian meal 🍫Chocolate: Neuhaus or Pierre Marcolini — world-class Belgian pralines
Evening
18:30

Sablon & Belgian Ales

  • Place du Grand Sablon — antiques, chocolatiers, lovely tree-lined square
  • Église Notre-Dame du Sablon — stunning Gothic interior, free entry
  • Ixelles neighbourhood for a local, vibrant evening
🍺Delirium Café — 2,000+ beers, required stop 🦐Grey shrimp croquettes — the definitive Belgian starter

Tip: Get a Liège waffle (dense, caramelised pearl sugar) from a street stand — not the flat Brussels kind sold to tourists. It's one of the best things you'll eat on this entire trip.

Day 10

Brussels → Amsterdam & Departure

Fri, Sep 5 – Sat, Sep 6 Amsterdam
🚄

Bruxelles-Midi → Amsterdam Centraal

Thalys or IC direct · Book via NS International · depart ~12:00

~1 hr 50 min

Schedule

Morning
09:00

Final Brussels Morning

  • Atomium exterior — the iconic 1958 World's Fair structure
  • One last Liège waffle at a street stand near Grand-Place
  • Pick up Belgian chocolates or Trappist beers for home
Midday
12:00

Travel to Amsterdam

Board the Thalys. Arrive Amsterdam Centraal ~13:50. Check in to your final night hotel near Schiphol or the centre.

Afternoon
15:00

Farewell Amsterdam Afternoon

  • Last souvenir run — Delftware, stroopwafels, Dutch gin (jenever)
  • FOAM Photography Museum — consistently excellent rotating exhibitions
  • One final canal-side café for bitterballen and a cold Heineken
Evening
19:30

Farewell Dinner & Early Night

  • Dinner to celebrate 10 extraordinary days across Europe
  • Early night — flight at 11:00, aim for Schiphol by 08:30
🍽Restaurant Vinkeles — Michelin-starred in a 17th-century convent bakery 🥩Budget pick: Rijsel — superb Belgian-Dutch fusion in Amsterdam Oost
Sep 6
08:00

✈ Depart Amsterdam Schiphol — 11:00

Sprinter from Amsterdam Centraal takes 17 min to Schiphol. Be at check-in by 08:30 at the latest. Safe travels — what an incredible journey.

Tip: Schiphol has a Rijksmuseum annex inside the terminal (airside, past security) — a perfect parting gift if you clear security with time to spare.
