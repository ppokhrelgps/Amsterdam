Rise Early — Pack for the Summit

Breakfast at hotel or grab supplies from a supermarket the night before. Pack: sunscreen (UV is intense at altitude), sunglasses, a warm mid-layer (it's 0–5°C at the top even in August), and your Eurail pass. Check the Jungfraujoch webcam — if summit is clouded over, swap with Day 5 (Schilthorn is at lower altitude and often clearer).

depart

🚄 Interlaken Ost → Grindelwald Terminal — Eurail free

BLS train — show your Eurail pass, no ticket purchase needed. ~35-minute scenic ride through the valley. Arrive Grindelwald Terminal ~07:35.

07:45–
09:30

🏔 Eiger Express Gondola + Jungfraubahn to Summit

  • At Grindelwald Terminal: present your Eurail pass at the Jungfraujoch ticket window — pay CHF ~196/person (25% off the CHF 261 full price) + CHF 10 seat reservation
  • Eiger Express gondola — 15-min cable car flight across the Eiger's north face to Eigergletscher
  • Jungfraubahn rack railway through the tunnel bored inside the Eiger — stops at Eigerwand and Eismeer viewpoints (5 min each)
  • Arrive Jungfraujoch station at 3,454 m — Top of Europe
09:30–
13:00

At the Summit — 3,454 m

  • Sphinx Observatory terrace — 360° panorama: Aletsch Glacier (longest in the Alps, 23 km), peaks of the Bernese Oberland
  • Ice Palace — carved glacier caves with ice sculptures; ethereally lit and free once you're up
  • Snow Fun Park — sledging, snow tubing, and a zip line in August; genuinely extraordinary novelty
  • Plateau Trail — short walking path along the snow field with unobstructed glacier views
  • Sphinxterrace viewing deck — the highest viewpoint, accessible by lift from inside
🍜Lunch: Crystal Restaurant at the summit — pricey but the setting is once-in-a-lifetime · or bring your own packed lunch and eat on the Plateau
13:00–
15:00

Descent via Kleine Scheidegg → Lauterbrunnen — Eurail free

  • Take the Jungfraubahn back down to Kleine Scheidegg (the mountain saddle with views of all three peaks)
  • Change train at Kleine Scheidegg — descend via Wengen and Lauterbrunnen (different valley from the ascent — see both sides of the mountain)
  • All trains from Kleine Scheidegg down are Eurail covered — just show your pass, no ticket needed
15:00–
17:00

Lauterbrunnen Valley Afternoon — Free

  • Staubbach Falls — 300 m waterfall cascading straight off the cliff face into the valley; free, walk right up to it
  • Walk the valley floor trail — 72 waterfalls, wildflowers, towering limestone walls on both sides
  • Lauterbrunnen village itself is picturesque and relaxed after the summit intensity
☕Coffee stop: Airtime Café in Lauterbrunnen — good coffee, valley views
17:00–
17:30

🚄 Lauterbrunnen → Interlaken Ost — Eurail free

BLS train, ~20 minutes. Show Eurail pass. Back in Interlaken by 17:30.

18:00–
21:00

Rest & Dinner

You've been at altitude all day — rest, hydrate, and eat well. Early night: Schilthorn tomorrow departs at 08:00.

🫕Dinner: Fondue at Restaurant Tälli or pick up groceries from Coop for a quiet night in

Tip: Book your Jungfraujoch ticket online at jungfrau.ch before the trip — the 25% Eurail discount is applied at the ticket window when you show your pass, but having a time-slot reservation locked in means you won't miss the morning gondola in peak August season. The first departure from Grindelwald fills up fast.

🚌 PostBus: Lauterbrunnen → Stechelberg — Eurail free

The yellow PostBus runs every 30 minutes from outside Lauterbrunnen station to Stechelberg (the base cable car station for Schilthorn). Show Eurail pass — fully covered, no ticket needed. ~15-minute ride through the waterfall valley.

09:00–
12:30

🚡 Schilthorn Cable Car — 25% off with Eurail

  • At Stechelberg base station: show Eurail pass — pay CHF ~86/person return (25% off full CHF 115)
  • Four-stage cable car: Stechelberg → Gimmelwald → Mürren → Birg → Piz Gloria (Schilthorn summit, 2,970 m)
  • Stop at Birg on the way up — the Thrill Walk (a cliff-face walkway with glass floor) is free and spectacular
  • Piz Gloria revolving restaurant — filmed the 1969 James Bond film; one full rotation takes ~55 min
  • James Bond World exhibition — interactive displays, film props, great views from the panorama deck
  • 360° panorama: Eiger, Mönch, Jungfrau, and on clear days Mont Blanc and the Black Forest
🍽Lunch: Piz Gloria revolving restaurant — eat one rotation, then descend · budget CHF 30–45
12:30–
14:30

Mürren — Car-Free Clifftop Village — Eurail free

  • Stop at Mürren on the descent (exit at Mürren cable car station, not Stechelberg)
  • One of Switzerland's most beautiful villages — 1,650 m, no roads, no cars, ever
  • Northface Trail — 4 km walk with the Eiger's legendary north face filling the entire skyline; free, no ticket
  • Walk from Mürren down to Gimmelwald (~30 min downhill) if energy allows — arguably the best short hike in the Oberland
  • To return to Interlaken from Mürren: cable car to Grütschalp (Eurail covered) → BLS train to Interlaken (Eurail covered)
14:30–
16:00

💧 Trümmelbach Falls — CHF 14 (no Eurail discount)

  • On the way back from Mürren, stop at Trümmelbach (accessible by PostBus — Eurail covered)
  • 10 glacial waterfalls carving through the inside of the mountain — a lift takes you into the gorge
  • Thunderously powerful, dramatically lit, and completely unlike anything else on the trip
  • Pay at door: CHF 14/person — no Eurail discount but absolutely worth it
16:30–
17:00

🚄 Lauterbrunnen → Interlaken Ost — Eurail free

BLS train, 20 minutes. Show Eurail pass. Back in Interlaken by 17:00.

17:00–
21:00

Final Alps Evening — Pack for Paris

  • If skies are clear: sit on the Höheweg with a beer and watch alpenglow turn the Jungfrau pink — unforgettable last image
  • Pack tonight — departure train tomorrow at 10:59, aim for Interlaken Ost by 10:40
  • Pick up Swiss chocolate from Coop for friends back home
🫕Farewell fondue: Restaurant Schuh or Zum Bären — end Switzerland on a classic

Tip: For Schilthorn, no advance booking required — just show up at Stechelberg and show your Eurail pass at the ticket window for the 25% discount. However, arrive by 09:00 in August as morning cable cars fill up with day-trippers. Mürren itself is free to walk around — the cable car from Grütschalp to Mürren is Eurail-covered so there's no extra charge to enter the village.

Verdict: Stick with Eurail only. Here's why.
  • Swiss Travel Pass saves you ~CHF 33 on Jungfraujoch vs Eurail, but costs CHF 250+ to buy — a net loss
  • Jungfrau Travel Pass would be useful only if you skip Jungfraujoch summit and do many local excursions — not your plan
