Day 01

Amsterdam — The Essential Day

Wed, Aug 27 Amsterdam

Schedule

Morning
10:20

✈ Arrive at Schiphol (AMS)

Clear customs, collect bags. Sprinter train to Amsterdam Centraal (~17 min, €5.50). Check in or drop bags at hotel — most allow early luggage drop. Grab a coffee and get moving.

☕Breakfast: Scandinavian Embassy — the city's best specialty coffee
Afternoon
12:30

Jordaan, Canals & the Big Highlights

  • Anne Frank House — book timed entry in advance (annefrank.org), absolutely non-negotiable
  • Prinsengracht & Keizersgracht canal walk — the classic Amsterdam scene
  • Rijksmuseum — Rembrandt, Vermeer, Dutch Golden Age; allow 90 min (pre-book)
  • Albert Cuyp Market in De Pijp — stroopwafels, street food, atmosphere
🥩Lunch: Moeders — hearty Dutch comfort food, Jordaan 🐟Snack: raw herring at a Haring cart
Evening
19:00

Canal Cruise & Leidseplein

  • Evening canal cruise — golden-hour light on the grachten is unmissable
  • Leidseplein square — café terraces, street performers, great energy
  • Brouwerij 't IJ — windmill craft brewery, a perfect nightcap
🍽Dinner: De Silveren Spiegel — refined Dutch cuisine in a 1614 canal house

Tip: One focused day is exactly enough. Prioritise Anne Frank House + Rijksmuseum + a canal cruise and you'll leave satisfied. Buy an OV-chipkaart for trams and buses.

Cologne → Interlaken Alps Base

Fri, Aug 29 Swiss Alps

Köln Hbf → Basel → Interlaken Ost

Schedule

Morning
08:00

Depart Cologne

The leg from Basel onward is one of Europe's most scenic train rides — you enter the Bernese Oberland and the Alps unfold around you. Arrive Interlaken Ost ~13:30. Interlaken is your mountain base for three nights.

Afternoon
14:00

Interlaken & Lake Thun

  • Höheweg promenade — the famous strip between Lakes Thun and Brienz with the Eiger, Mönch and Jungfrau directly ahead
  • Boat cruise on Lake Thun — glassy water, Alpine reflections, castle silhouettes
  • Spiez village (optional short stop) — beautiful lakeside castle
🧀Lunch: Käseschnitte (Swiss cheese toast) at a lakeside café
Evening
19:00

Settle In — Plan the Alps Days

Easy first evening. Check the Jungfraujoch webcam (jungfrau.ch), review tomorrow's route, set an early alarm, and rest. The mountains demand early starts.

🍷Dinner: Restaurant Schuh — elegant terrace overlooking the Höheweg

Tip: The Jungfrau Travel Pass covers regional trains, boats and cable cars across the region — compare it against point-to-point tickets for your specific days, as it can save a substantial amount.

Day 04

Jungfraujoch — Top of Europe 3,454 m

Sat, Aug 30 Swiss Alps

Schedule

Morning
07:00

Early Departure — First Trains Are Best

Train from Interlaken Ost → Grindelwald or Lauterbrunnen → Kleine Scheidegg → Jungfraujoch. The rack railway through the Eiger tunnel is an experience on its own. Allow 2.5 hours each way.

  • Target the ~07:35 departure — best visibility, fewest crowds, best light
  • Book at jungfrau.ch in advance — CHF 145–215 depending on season and pass
☕Breakfast on the train or at Kleine Scheidegg station
Late Morn
10:00

At the Summit (3,454 m)

  • Sphinx Observatory — 360° panorama over the Aletsch Glacier, the longest glacier in the Alps
  • Snow Fun Park — sledging and snowball fights in August, extraordinary novelty
  • Ice Palace — carved glacier tunnels with ice sculptures
  • Plateau Trail — walking path along the snow with views of peaks and ice in all directions
🍜Lunch: Crystal Restaurant at the top — pricey but the setting is extraordinary
Afternoon
14:00

Descent via Grindelwald

  • Return via Grindelwald — gorgeous village directly under the Eiger north face
  • Stroll the village, pick up local cheese, browse the shops
  • Grindelwald First cable car (optional, extra fee) — clifftop walks and mountain cart rides
Evening
19:30

Return to Interlaken

Back in town by evening. Rest well — Schilthorn tomorrow is another big day.

🥩Dinner: Laterne Restaurant — warm, unpretentious Swiss mountain food

Tip: Check the Jungfraujoch live webcam the morning you go. If the summit is socked in with cloud, swap Day 4 and Day 5 — Schilthorn at a lower elevation often has better visibility on overcast days.

Day 05

Schilthorn & Lauterbrunnen Valley 2,970 m

Sun, Aug 31 Swiss Alps

Schedule

Morning
08:00

Lauterbrunnen Valley

  • Train from Interlaken Ost to Lauterbrunnen (~20 min)
  • Staubbach Falls — 300 m waterfall plunging straight off the cliff into the valley, free and breathtaking
  • Trümmelbach Falls — glacial meltwater carving through the mountain interior (CHF ~14 at the door)
  • Walk the valley floor trail between waterfalls — the valley has 72 of them
🥐Breakfast: Airtime Café, Lauterbrunnen — great views, good coffee
Midday
11:30

Schilthorn — Piz Gloria (2,970 m)

  • Cable car from Stechelberg via Gimmelwald and Mürren to the summit
  • Piz Gloria revolving restaurant — filming location for the 1969 James Bond film On Her Majesty's Secret Service
  • Panorama: Eiger, Mönch, Jungfrau, and on clear days all the way to Mont Blanc and the Black Forest
  • James Bond World exhibition — fun interactive displays about the filming
🍽Lunch: Piz Gloria rotating restaurant — one slow revolution while you eat
Afternoon
15:00

Mürren — Car-Free Clifftop Village

  • Stop at Mürren on the descent — one of Switzerland's most beautiful villages, clinging to a cliff at 1,650 m, no cars ever
  • Northface Trail — a 4 km walk with the Eiger's legendary north face filling the entire skyline
  • Sit on a bench, breathe the mountain air — this is the Switzerland you came for
  • Optional: walk from Mürren down to Gimmelwald (30 min, one of Europe's great short hikes)
Evening
19:00

Final Alps Evening in Interlaken

Return to Interlaken. Pack for Paris tomorrow. If skies are clear, sit outside — watch the Eiger and Jungfrau turn pink at alpenglow. It's a moment you'll remember.

🫕Farewell fondue: Restaurant Tälli, Interlaken — end on a classic

Tip: August is peak paragliding season in Interlaken — if that's on your bucket list, operators in Lauterbrunnen can arrange tandem jumps with landing in the Interlaken valley. Unforgettable views on the way down.

Verdict: Stick with Eurail only. Here's why.
  • Swiss Travel Pass saves you ~CHF 33 on Jungfraujoch vs Eurail, but costs CHF 250+ to buy — a net loss
  • Jungfrau Travel Pass would be useful only if you skip Jungfraujoch summit and do many local excursions — not your plan
  • Your 7-day Eurail pass handles all train travel free, and mountain discounts don't consume a travel day
  • Use 1 Eurail travel day: Interlaken → Paris (Sep 1). Save remaining days for Germany/Belgium legs
  • Total mountain out-of-pocket with Eurail: ~CHF 315 (Jungfraujoch + Schilthorn + Harder Kulm)
