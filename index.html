Amsterdam → Cologne → Zurich → Paris → Brussels → Amsterdam Arrive Aug 27 · Depart Sep 6 at 11:00
Day 01

Arrival in Amsterdam

Wed, Aug 27 Amsterdam

Schedule

Morning
10:20

✈ Land at Amsterdam Schiphol (AMS)

Clear customs, collect luggage. Take the Sprinter or Intercity train from Schiphol Airport directly to Amsterdam Centraal (~17 min, €5.50). Check in to your hotel and freshen up.

Afternoon
13:00

Jordaan & Canal Belt

  • Stroll the Jordaan neighbourhood — cobbled lanes, independent boutiques
  • Walk along Prinsengracht and Keizersgracht canals
  • Visit the Anne Frank House (book tickets in advance!)
🍽Lunch at Moeders (Dutch comfort food)
Evening
18:00

Leidseplein & Dinner

  • Evening canal cruise for a golden-hour view of the grachten
  • Explore Leidseplein square — lively café terraces
🍺Brouwerij 't IJ (windmill brewery) 🥩De Silveren Spiegel for Dutch cuisine

Tip: Buy an OV-chipkaart transit card at Schiphol — reusable for trams, metro, and buses throughout your stay.

Day 02

Amsterdam Deep Dive

Thu, Aug 28 Amsterdam

Schedule

Morning
09:00

Museumplein

  • Rijksmuseum — Rembrandt, Vermeer, and Dutch Golden Age masterworks (allow 2–3 hrs)
  • Van Gogh Museum (book ahead) — the world's largest Van Gogh collection
☕Breakfast at Scandinavian Embassy (specialty coffee)
Afternoon
14:00

Vondelpark & De Pijp

  • Picnic or walk through Vondelpark — Amsterdam's beloved green lung
  • Albert Cuyp Market — largest outdoor market in Europe
  • Heineken Experience (optional, fun brewery tour)
🥙Lunch at Bazar Amsterdam (Middle Eastern)
Evening
19:00

Nieuwmarkt & Red Light District

  • Wander the historic Nieuwmarkt and De Wallen
  • Visit Oude Kerk — Amsterdam's oldest church amid the nightlife
🐟Raw herring at a street stand (stroopwafel for dessert) 🍷Dinner at Bistrot Neuf
Day 03

Amsterdam → Cologne

Fri, Aug 29 Cologne
🚄

Amsterdam Centraal → Köln Hbf

ICE or Thalys high-speed train · Book via NS International or DB

~2 hr 40 min

Schedule

Morning
08:00

Depart Amsterdam

Board an early ICE train (~08:00–08:30 departure). Enjoy the Rhine countryside as you arrive in Cologne mid-morning. Check in and drop bags.

Afternoon
12:00

Cologne Cathedral & Old Town

  • Kölner Dom (Cathedral) — climb the South Tower for panoramic Rhine views (533 steps)
  • Roman-Germanic Museum — ancient history right next to the Dom
  • Stroll the Altstadt along the Rhine riverfront promenade
🍺Kölsch beer at Früh am Dom (traditional Brauhaus) 🥨Lunch: Himmel un Ääd (local potato & blood sausage dish)
Evening
18:30

Hohenzollern Bridge & Rhine Walk

  • Love locks on the Hohenzollern Bridge at sunset
  • Explore the Belgian Quarter (Belgisches Viertel) — indie bars, boutiques
🍷Dinner at Em Krützche (cozy Rhine-view restaurant)

Tip: The Cologne Card offers unlimited public transit + museum discounts. Grab one at the main station tourist office.

Day 04

Cologne → Zurich

Sat, Aug 30 Zurich
🚄

Köln Hbf → Zürich HB

ICE direct via Frankfurt or with one change · Book via DB (Deutsche Bahn)

~4 hr 30 min

Schedule

Morning
08:30

Depart Cologne

Board the morning train. The route passes through the Rhine Valley — grab a window seat. Arrive in Zurich ~13:00. Check in and get oriented on the lakefront.

Afternoon
14:00

Zurich Old Town (Altstadt)

  • Grossmünster and Fraumünster churches — Chagall windows in Fraumünster
  • Bahnhofstrasse luxury shopping strip (window shop or splurge)
  • Lindenhügel hill — free panoramic views of the city and Alps
🧀Fondue lunch at Raclette Factory
Evening
18:30

Lake Zurich Promenade

  • Sunset stroll along Zürichsee — locals swim in summer, join them!
  • Explore Langstrasse district — vibrant nightlife, street art
🍽Dinner at Kronenhalle (classic Zurich brasserie, Picasso on the walls)
Day 05

Swiss Alps Day Trip

Sun, Aug 31 Zurich

Schedule

Morning
07:30

Day Trip to Lucerne & Mount Pilatus

  • Train from Zurich HB to Lucerne (~50 min, every 30 min)
  • Chapel Bridge (Kapellbrücke) — world's oldest covered wooden bridge
  • Lake Lucerne boat cruise or scenic walk along the waterfront
☕Breakfast on the train or at Lucerne station
Afternoon
13:00

Mount Pilatus (Optional)

  • Cog railway up to Pilatus Kulm (2,132m) — world's steepest rack railway
  • Panoramic views of the Alps and 73 mountain peaks on clear days
  • Return via gondola and bus — "Golden Round Trip" route
🍫Swiss chocolate tasting at Hotel Pilatus-Kulm
Evening
19:00

Return to Zurich, Rest Evening

Return train to Zurich. Easy evening — recover and pack for Paris tomorrow.

🥩Zürcher Geschnetzeltes (veal in cream) at Zeughauskeller

Tip: The Swiss Travel Pass covers trains, boats, buses, and most mountain railways. Check if it's worth it vs point-to-point tickets for your route.

Eiffel Tower at Dusk

  • Trocadéro terrace for the classic Eiffel Tower photo at golden hour
  • Champ de Mars park picnic or restaurant

🍷Dinner at Le Jules Verne (inside the Tower — book well ahead) 🥖Budget option: Au Pied de Fouet (classic bistro)
Day 07

Paris: Grands Musées

Tue, Sep 2 Paris

Schedule

Morning
09:00

The Louvre

  • Mona Lisa, Venus de Milo, Winged Victory — allow 3 hours minimum
  • Book skip-the-line tickets online (mandatory)
  • Cour Napoléon glass pyramid — iconic architecture
☕Early breakfast at Café Marly overlooking the pyramid
Afternoon
14:00

Musée d'Orsay & Saint-Germain

  • Musée d'Orsay — Monet, Renoir, Degas in a converted railway station
  • Stroll Saint-Germain-des-Prés — literary cafés, Sartre and Simone de Beauvoir haunts
  • Browse Marché aux Puces if energy allows
🥗Lunch at Café de Flore or Les Deux Magots
Evening
19:30

Montmartre

  • Sacré-Cœur Basilica — panoramic view of Paris at dusk
  • Place du Tertre — artists at work under lamplight
  • Le Moulin Rouge exterior (quintessentially Parisian)
🧅French onion soup at La Bonne Franquette 🍮Crème brûlée to finish

Tip: The Paris Museum Pass (2 or 4 days) covers the Louvre, Orsay, Versailles, and 50+ sites — excellent value if you're museum-hopping.

Seine Cruise & Last Paris Night

Day 09

Paris → Brussels

Thu, Sep 4 Brussels
🚄

Paris Gare du Nord → Bruxelles-Midi

Thalys or Eurostar high-speed train · Book via Thalys or Rail Europe

~1 hr 25 min

Schedule

Morning
10:00

Relaxed Departure from Paris

Take a mid-morning Thalys. Arrive Brussels by noon. This is one of Europe's fastest rail connections — leave Paris late, arrive early. Check in near Grand-Place.

🥐Grab a final Parisian croissant before boarding
Afternoon
13:00

Grand-Place & Historic Centre

  • Grand-Place — one of Europe's most beautiful main squares (UNESCO)
  • Manneken Pis — surprisingly tiny, surprisingly beloved
  • Galeries Royales Saint-Hubert — 19th-century covered arcade
  • Magritte Museum (dedicated to the Belgian surrealist)
🍟Moules-frites at Aux Armes de Bruxelles 🍫Neuhaus or Pierre Marcolini for world-class Belgian chocolate
Evening
18:30

Ixelles & Sablon

  • Place du Grand Sablon — antique dealers, upscale chocolatiers
  • Église Notre-Dame du Sablon (beautiful Gothic church)
  • Ixelles neighbourhood — vibrant student bars and EU district buzz
🍺Belgian ales at Delirium Café (2,000+ beers on menu) 🦐Grey shrimp croquettes — the ultimate Belgian starter

Tip: Brussels is underrated — hotel prices are lower than Paris, food is excellent, and the waffle vs. gaufre debate is very real. Get both.
